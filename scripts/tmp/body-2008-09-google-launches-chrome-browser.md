Today Google launched its own first web browser called [Chrome](http://www.google.com/chrome/?hl=en). Less is more, Chrome comes with a very nice lean design.

[
![image](images/image-thumb.png)
](https://www.verboon.info/wp-content/uploads/2008/09/image.png)

Chrome has a couple of nice [features](http://www.google.com/chrome/intl/en/features.html?hl=en), one I personally found quite interesting is that the address bar also acts as the search bar. As you type in words in the address bar, you receive instant search results.

[
![image](images/image-thumb1.png)
](https://www.verboon.info/wp-content/uploads/2008/09/image1.png)

I'm quite sure that the chrome browser will get quickly adopted by home users, as we have seen previously happening with the [Firefox](http://www.mozilla.com/en-US/firefox/) browser, but I'm curious to see if this browser will become a serious competitor within the enterprise space, where [Internet Explorer still plays a dominant role](http://www.populationstatistic.com/archives/2008/09/01/google-chrome-a-browser-without-a-home/) and most likely will continue to do so.

As always...... give it a try.
