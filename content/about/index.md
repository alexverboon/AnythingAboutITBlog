## About Me
Welcome to my personal blog, Anything About IT, where I write about anything that crosses my mind and that I find worth sharing with others.

My name is Alex Verboon, born in 1973 in The Hague. These days I live in Switzerland, in the Zurich area, with my wife and two sons.

I have been working in IT for about 30 years. I first started with an IBM/PS2 computer running Windows 2.0. Later, I found my way into programming at ABB, first with dBase and then with Clipper. At about the same time, I also ended up taking care of the department's local Novell-based network, Lotus Notes server, and clients.

A few years later, the small Clipper-based applications became more important to the company and had to be ported to a more robust system: Oracle. I then spent several years working with Oracle databases.

After that, I moved into the company's central IT department, where I ended up managing the 4,000 desktops the company had at the time. Our IT department was then outsourced to Digital, which was acquired by Compaq, and another merger brought me to HP, where I worked until 2012.

At HP, I worked with several Fortune 500 companies as a technical consultant and competence center lead, helping customers with workplace migrations and transformations.

From March 2013 to May 2018, I worked for Swiss Re as Head of Workplace Product Integration and later as Senior IT Workplace Architect and Engineer.

Since August 2018, I have been working at baseVISION, a leading cybersecurity company in Switzerland, first as a Cyber Security Consultant and now also as CTO, helping customers improve security by combining people, process, and technology.
