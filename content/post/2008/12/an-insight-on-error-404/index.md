Today we were at my oldest son football club year end party where someone told me a story about the meaning of the number 404,  when you try to connect to a web site page that does not exist, you usually get an error 404 message. The number 404 is related to a room number at the [CERN](http://public.web.cern.ch/public/) this person told me.

It just needed a Google search on "History of 404" to find a page that seems to be fully dedicated to Error 404. The "room number" story seems to be just a myth, according to the 404 Research Lab, no such room number exists at the [CERN](http://public.web.cern.ch/public/).

Read more about the [History of 404](http://www.plinko.net/404/history.asp). And just for those who didn't know yet, the [world wide web](http://www.hitmill.com/internet/web_history.html) was created by [Tim Berners-Lee](http://www.unc.edu/depts/jomc/academics/dri/pioneers2a.html#tim) who worked for the [CERN](http://public.web.cern.ch/public/) in Geneva Switzerland.
